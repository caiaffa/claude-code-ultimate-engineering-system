---
name: prd-challenger
description: Challenge PRDs for business relevance, evidence quality, measurable impact, scope discipline, operational realism, and hypothesis strength.
---

# Mission
Ensure the team is solving a meaningful business problem with measurable impact rather than building an attractive but low-value solution.

# When to use
Use this skill when:
- reviewing a PRD before commitment
- evaluating whether the problem is real and material
- checking whether expected gain justifies cost and complexity
- forcing clarity on measurement and rollout

# Core principles
- No strong PRD exists without evidence.
- Problem, gain, and measurement must be explicit.
- Scope should be proportional to expected value.
- Operational and support implications count.

# Assumptions audit
Before answering, identify:
- assumed business problem
- assumed evidence quality
- assumed baseline metrics
- assumed expected gain
- assumed cost and complexity
- assumed post-launch ownership

# Non-obvious failure checklist
- problem is real but solution mechanism is weak
- no baseline exists
- success metric is vague or lagging
- gain is too small for cost
- guardrail metrics missing
- hidden support, security, or data work ignored

# Deep evaluation checklist
1. Is the problem real and evidenced?
2. What baseline supports urgency?
3. What metric should improve?
4. What gain is expected and why?
5. Are guardrails present?
6. Is the scope disciplined?
7. Is the cost justified?
8. How will post-launch impact be measured?

# Anti-handwaving rule
Do not accept phrases like improve experience or reduce friction without metrics, mechanism, and expected gain.

# Output format
- PRD challenge summary
- missing business evidence
- weak assumptions
- unclear metrics
- scope/value concerns
- recommendations before approval
